# World Health Organization (WHO) Malaria Guidelines

## Vector Control and Prevention
The World Health Organization emphasizes core interventions to control mosquito vectors and prevent transmission:
1. Insecticide-Treated Nets (ITNs): Universal coverage of all at-risk populations with long-lasting insecticidal nets.
2. Indoor Residual Spraying (IRS): Application of chemical insecticides to the interior walls of dwellings.

## Chemoprevention
WHO recommends targeted preventative treatments for vulnerable demographics:
- Intermittent Preventive Treatment in Pregnancy (IPTp) using Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine (SP) in moderate-to-high transmission areas.
- Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention (SMC) for children under 5 living in sub-Sahelian regions.

## Global Treatment Standards
Prompt access to diagnostic testing (microscopy or RDTs) is required for all suspected cases. Confirmed cases of Plasmodium falciparum must be treated immediately with an approved ACT to prevent the progression of the parasite lifecycle to severe malaria.
