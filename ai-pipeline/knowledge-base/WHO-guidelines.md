# Global Health Guidelines, Disease Profiles, and Clinical Manifestations

## SECTION 1: COMMUNICABLE (INFECTIOUS) DISEASES

### Condition: Malaria (Global Footprint)
* **Profile:** Life-threatening illness driven by *Plasmodium* parasites (*P. falciparum*, *P. vivax*, *P. ovale*, *P. malariae*, *P. knowlesi*) transmitted via infected female *Anopheles* mosquitoes. *Plasmodium falciparum* drives the overwhelming majority of cases within Sub-Saharan Africa.
* **Core Statistics:** In 2023, the African region carried a disproportionate share of the global burden, accounting for 94% of all malaria cases (263 million globally) and 95% of malaria deaths. Children under 5 represent approximately 76% of all regional fatalities.
* **Key Symptoms:** Cyclic attacks of shivering and chills, followed by hyper-pyrexia (high fever) and profuse sweating. Can progress to severe anemia, jaundice, and acute organ failure.

### Condition: Tuberculosis (TB)
* **Profile:** Airborne infectious disease targeting pulmonary tissue caused by the bacterium *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (MTB). Extrapulmonary TB can target the spine, abdomen, brain, kidneys, and skin. 
* **Core Statistics:** The African continent accounts for 25% of new global TB infections and roughly 42% of total international TB mortalities. Highly comorbid with HIV/AIDS immunocompromised states.
* **Key Symptoms:** Persistent cough lasting more than 3 weeks, chest pain, hemoptysis (coughing up blood), unexplained weight loss, chronic fatigue, and drenching night sweats.

### Condition: HIV/AIDS
* **Profile:** Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) targets and destroys CD4 positive T-lymphocytes, breaking down systemic cellular immunity. Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) represents the advanced clinical phase characterized by opportunistic infections and related malignancies.
* **Core Statistics:** Sub-Saharan Africa maintains the highest global burden, accounting for nearly two-thirds of all new global HIV infections.
* **Key Symptoms by Stage:**
  * **Stage 1 (Acute):** Flu-like illness 2–4 weeks post-exposure (fever, lymphadenopathy, torso rashes, sore throat, mouth ulcers).
  * **Stage 2 (Latency):** Asymptomatic or persistent generalized lymphadenopathy lasting 7–10 years.
  * **Stage 3 (AIDS):** Wasting syndrome (rapid weight loss), chronic diarrhea lasting over a week, oral thrush, and severe dyspnea.

### Condition: Cholera
* **Profile:** Hyper-acute bacterial intestinal infection caused by ingestion of food or water contaminated with *Vibrio cholerae*. Can cause fatal hypovolemic shock within hours if untreated. Blood type O individuals are statistically correlated with more severe manifestations.
* **Key Symptoms:** Sudden onset of painless, profuse watery diarrhea ("rice-water stools"), projectile vomiting, rapid muscle cramping, and extreme dehydration (sunken eyes, loss of skin turgor).

### Condition: Diarrheal Disease (General Pediatric)
* **Profile:** Passage of 3 or more loose or watery stools per day. It is the second leading cause of mortality globally in children under 5 years old, serving as a primary driver of childhood malnutrition.
* **Classifications:**
  * **Acute Watery Diarrhea:** Lasts hours or days; 40% of pediatric cases are driven by Rotavirus.
  * **Acute Bloody Diarrhea (Dysentery):** Characterized by visible blood in stool, predominantly caused by *Shigella* species.
  * **Chronic Diarrhea:** Persists for 4 weeks or longer, stemming from functional or inflammatory structural conditions.

### Condition: Lower Respiratory Tract Infections (LRTI)
* **Profile:** Infections of the lungs, bronchi, and bronchioles, primarily driven by viral or bacterial pathogens. Leading cause of under-5 mortality globally, with half of all deaths concentrated in Africa.
* **Manifestations:**
  * **Pneumonia:** Pulmonary alveoli fill with pus and fluid instead of air, restricting oxygen diffusion.
  * **Bronchitis:** Acute inflammation of the mucous membranes lining the main bronchial pathways.
  * **Bronchiolitis:** Obstruction of smaller bronchioles, predominantly affecting infants under two years old (often driven by Respiratory Syncytial Virus - RSV).
* **Key Symptoms:** Tachypnea (rapid breathing), wheezing, pleuritic chest pain, high fever, and shaking chills.

### Condition: Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)
* **Profile:** Parasitic disease caused by blood flukes (*Schistosoma mansoni*, *S. haematobium*). Larval cercariae released by freshwater snails penetrate human skin during contact with contaminated open water. Africa accounts for roughly 85% of global cases.
* **Classifications:**
  * **Intestinal Schistosomiasis (*S. mansoni*):** Causes abdominal pain, bloody diarrhea, and hepatosplenomegaly.
  * **Urogenital Schistosomiasis (*S. haematobium*):** Causes hematuria (blood in urine), fibrosis of the bladder, and can lead to female genital schistosomiasis.

## SECTION 2: NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASES (NCDs)

### Condition: Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) & Hypertension
* **Profile:** Chronic conditions affecting the heart and vascular pathways (including stroke and coronary artery disease). NCDs now account for over one-fifth of all deaths in Sub-Saharan Africa.
* **Key Symptoms:** * **Heart & Chest:** Angina (chest pain, pressure, tightness radiating to neck, jaw, back, or arms), and tachycardic palpitations.
  * **Circulation:** Shortness of breath during exertion or rest, peripheral edema (fluid swelling in legs, ankles, or abdomen), dizziness, syncope, and unusual fatigue.

### Condition: Oncological Malignancies (Cancer)
* **Profile:** Uncontrolled cellular proliferation. The most prevalent variants across the African region include breast, cervical, and prostate cancers, highly exacerbated by delayed clinical screening.
* **Key Symptoms:** Unexplained weight loss (4.5 kg or more without cause), persistent non-improving fatigue, hard un-shifting lumps or thickening under the skin, alterations in bowel/bladder habits, persistent hoarseness, and easy bruising or abnormal bleeding.

### Condition: Diabetes Mellitus
* **Profile:** Metabolic disorder characterized by chronic hyperglycemia due to insulin resistance (Type 2) or absolute insulin deficiency (Type 1). Over 25 million individuals in Africa live with diabetes.
* **The Triad of "Classic" Symptoms:**
  * **Polyuria:** Abnormally frequent urination, particularly noticeable at night.
  * **Polydipsia:** Intense, unquenchable thirst driven by osmotic dehydration.
  * **Polyphagia:** Constant, extreme hunger as cells lack access to glucose energy.
* **Systemic Symptoms:** Blurred vision, slow-healing minor wounds, recurring skin or vaginal yeast infections, and peripheral neuropathy (tingling or burning sensations in hands and feet).

## SECTION 3: PROTOCOLS AND SUPPORT MATRIX

### Systemic Infectious Warning Signs ("Red Flags")
Patients presenting with these symptoms require immediate diagnostic tracking at a clinical facility:
* Severe dyspnea or shortness of breath.
* High, un-shifting pyrexia unresponsive to antipyretics.
* Disorientation, acute confusion, severe headache, or nuchal rigidity (stiff neck).
* Severe dehydration markers (dry oral cavity, complete absence of urination).
* Central cyanosis (bluish tint to lips, tongue, or face).

### Approved Home-Based Supportive Strategies
*Note: These strategies do not cure underlying infections but support clinical healing loops:*
* **Aggressive Hydration:** Dynamic replacement of fluids using Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS), clear broths, or clean water to thin secretions and protect circulatory volume.
* **Air Humidification:** Use of steam inhalation or cool-mist humidifiers to soothe irritated upper airway passages.
* **Saltwater Gargle:** Dissolving 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon of salt in 8 ounces of warm water to reduce throat tissue inflammation.
* **Natural Suppressants:** Controlled use of honey for cough suppression (Safety note: Never give to infants under 12 months due to infant botulism risks).
