# Nigeria Public Health Guidelines & Facts

## Malaria Prevalence and Treatment
Malaria is a major public health challenge in Nigeria, accounting for a significant percentage of global cases. The primary cause of malaria is the Plasmodium falciparum parasite, transmitted through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito. 

### National Treatment Guidelines
- The Federal Ministry of Health recommends Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT) as the first-line treatment for uncomplicated malaria. Common examples include Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL) and Artesunate-Amodiaquine (AA).
- Diagnosis should be confirmed using a Rapid Diagnosis Test (RDT) or microscopy before administering treatment.
- Preventive measures include the deployment of Long-Lasting Insecticidal Nets (LLINs) and Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention (SMC) in high-transmission states.
